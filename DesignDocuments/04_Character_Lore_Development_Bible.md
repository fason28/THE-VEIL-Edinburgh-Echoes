# CHARACTER & LORE DEVELOPMENT BIBLE
## THE VEIL: Edinburgh Echoes

---

## PART 1: CHARACTER PROFILES

### PRIMARY CHARACTER: CASSIDY MARGARET "CASS" O'NEILL

#### Personal Background

**Full Name:** Cassidy Margaret "Cass" O'Neill  
**Age:** 32 years old (born 1892)  
**Birthplace:** Glasgow, Scotland  
**Occupation (Pre-Game):** British Army Sound Engineer, currently unemployed  
**Status:** Obsessive, haunted, driven by singular purpose  

#### Physical Description

- **Height:** 5'8" (173 cm)
- **Build:** Athletic but lean (result of constant movement and stress)
- **Hair:** Dark brown, usually unkempt
- **Eyes:** Gray-blue, often tired, occasionally intense with focus
- **Distinguishing Features:** Slight burn scar on left hand (WWI injury)
- **Typical Clothing:** Practical 1920s attire—dark colors, reinforced jacket, sturdy boots
- **Demeanor:** Quiet, purposeful, often appears distant or preoccupied
- **Voice:** Scottish-English accent (hybrid Glasgow/Edinburgh), clipped and direct speech

#### Psychological Profile

**Strengths:**
- Exceptional auditory processing (1% frequency accuracy)
- Deep technical proficiency (mechanical systems, electrical engineering)
- Determined and laser-focused under pressure
- Genuine empathy for lost souls
- Creative problem-solving
- Psychological resilience (survived WWI)

**Weaknesses:**
- Unresolved grief over Jacob's disappearance
- Obsessive-compulsive tendencies
- Severe isolation and trust issues
- PTSD manifestations (flashbacks, auditory hallucinations)
- Perfectionism bordering on dysfunction
- Tunnel vision when focused on goals

#### Detailed Backstory

**Early Life (1892-1910):**
- Born to working-class family in Glasgow
- Father: James O'Neill (steelworker, died 1905 in industrial accident)
- Mother: Margaret "Maggie" O'Neill (seamstress)
- Brother: Jacob O'Neill (2 years younger, born 1894, idolized older sibling)
- Cassidy became "the responsible one" after father's death at age 13
- Built makeshift radio receiver at age 12 (early technical aptitude)

**Military Service (1914-1919):**
- Joined British Army August 1914 (age 22)
- Specialized in acoustic location (early sonar technology)
- Role: Detecting enemy artillery positions via sound triangulation
- Deployed to Western Front (France), 1915
- Best friend "Tommy" killed during 1916 offensive (right next to Cassidy)
- Exposed to mustard gas (survived, lingering respiratory issues)
- Heard "ghost frequencies" that superiors dismissed
- Honorably discharged 1919 (marked as "sensitive" in medical record)

**Post-War Struggles (1919-1923):**
- Returned to Glasgow, lived with mother
- Could not find stable employment (employers skeptical of military background)
- Took odd jobs: dock work, factory repair, equipment maintenance
- Became obsessed with detecting "anomalous frequencies"
- Jacob wanted Cassidy to "move on"; growing estrangement
- **Mother's Death (1923):** Margaret died of pneumonia (age 61)
- Last conversation: Cassidy promised to "find Jacob and stop living in the past"

**Recent History (1924 - Game Start):**
- January 1924: Detected unusual frequency pattern (40-50 Hz) from Edinburgh
- Pattern matched "ghost phenomena" from WWI
- Sold mother's house to fund trip to Edinburgh
- Arrived March 1924 with modified equipment
- Began investigation, discovered Mary King's Close
- **Psychological State at Game Start:**
  - Obsessed but functional
  - Sleep-deprived, malnourished
  - Oscillates between hope and despair
  - Mild paranoia about government cover-ups

#### Unique Mechanic: Auditory Resonance

**The Ability:** Can distinguish frequency differences to within 0.5 Hz (normal human ~3 Hz)

**NOT a supernatural ability but result of:**
1. Natural aptitude (genetic predisposition for auditory processing)
2. Intensive training (military acoustic engineer)
3. Psychological obsession (repetitive focus sharpens ability)
4. Trauma-induced heightened perception

**Gameplay Impact:**
- Can tune frequencies with higher precision than others
- Can detect subtle changes in frequency field
- Can identify Echo type by acoustic signature
- Can hear Siphoner approach slightly earlier

#### Voice & Dialogue Characteristics

**Speech Pattern:**
- Clipped, direct, practical
- Often technical terminology
- Minimal small talk
- Scottish dialect subtly present

**Accent:** Glasgow/Edinburgh hybrid, softens over game (stress makes it more pronounced)

**Dialogue Tone:**
- Wry humor masking deep pain
- Sardonic observations
- Internal monologue frequent
- Increasingly vulnerable as game progresses

#### Character Arc

**Act I:** Denial → Investigation
- Confident Jacob is alive
- Discovers scope of phenomenon
- Experiences first Siphoner
- Realizes situation is bigger than expected

**Act II:** Acceptance → Understanding
- Resolves multiple Echoes
- Discovers Jacob partially consumed
- Must confront: he may be beyond saving
- Begins understanding loss more broadly

**Act III:** Resolution → Transformation
- Faces climactic choice
- Deepest motivation revealed (connection itself, not just Jacob)
- Final decision reflects psychological journey
- Character becomes emotionally mature

### SECONDARY CHARACTER: JACOB O'NEILL (THE ORB)

#### Character Essence

**Status:** Not dead, not alive—caught between states  
**Physical Form:** Glowing orb of ethereal light (blue-white spectrum)  
**Consciousness Level:** Partially aware, mostly reactive initially  
**Communication:** Via Jacob Orb mechanics and environmental reactions  

#### How Jacob Became The Orb

**The Incident (September 1923 - 6 months before game):**

1. Jacob discovers government frequency experiments
2. Believes he can understand what's happening
3. Enters sealed South Bridge vaults alone
4. Discovers apparatus designed to "bridge worlds"
5. Apparatus still broadcasting 40-50 Hz frequency
6. Jacob investigates closer to understand mechanism
7. **BREACH:** Accidentally activates apparatus (wrong frequency adjustment)
8. Reality tears; Veil becomes extremely thin
9. Primordial Siphoner begins crossing
10. Jacob becomes trapped in breach
11. His physical body remains; consciousness becomes trapped between states
12. Jacob's essence coalesces into orb form

#### Jacob's Current State

**Physical Form:**
- Orb of blue-white light, ~30cm diameter
- Visible in both Living World and Veil
- Can emit light, vibrate, change color
- Cannot speak directly (at game start)
- Cannot interact with physical objects

**Consciousness:**
- Fragmented memory (significant gaps)
- Aware of: Cassidy, locations, time progression
- Emotional responses: Fear, longing, protectiveness
- Limited control over manifestation
- Gradually regains coherence as Echoes resolve

**Memory Gaps:**
- Cannot remember his own death/transformation
- Uncertain how much time has passed
- Fuzzy memories of Cassidy
- Fragmented void-contact memories (disturbing)
- Unsure if he's alive or echoing

#### Jacob's Psychological Profile

**Pre-Incident Personality:**
- Curious, adventurous, sometimes reckless
- Spiritualist interests (séances, occult research)
- Loved Cassidy but frustrated with obsession
- Wanted to prove supernatural through evidence
- Romantic, believed in beauty of connection
- Naive about danger

**Current Personality (Orb State):**
- Protective (regarding Cassidy)
- Fearful (of Siphoners, dissolution)
- Lonely (isolated between states)
- Regretful (investigation was mistake)
- Hopeful (Cassidy's arrival feels like salvation)
- Confused (uncertain about nature of existence)

**Emotional Arc:**
- Act I: Fear and confusion (who is this woman?)
- Act II: Recognition and protection (growing certainty this is Cassidy)
- Act III: Acceptance and release (may not return to life)

#### Jacob's Hidden Knowledge

**What He Knows (Fragmented):**
1. Experiment was intentional, not accidental
2. Dr. Edmund Harlow orchestrated it
3. Purpose: Contact non-human intelligence (not just dead)
4. Siphoners exist beyond Veil
5. Breach is ongoing, slowly expanding
6. He may have been deliberately trapped (lure for someone else)

**What He's Unsure About:**
- Whether memories are real or implanted
- If still partially consumed by void entity
- If freeing him requires Cassidy's sacrifice
- Whether he wants to be freed

**What He Doesn't Know:**
- Cassidy searched 6 months for him
- Mother died while trapped
- Full extent of city-wide damage
- Cassidy's psychological deterioration

#### Jacob's Ultimate Mystery

**The Central Question:** Can Jacob be fully freed?

**Three Possibilities:**
1. **Complete Freedom:** Consciousness fully extracted, returns to life (requires Cassidy's sacrifice)
2. **Coexistence:** Remains Orb-bound but conscious and present (imperfect but honest)
3. **Dissolution:** Consciousness fragments, becomes another Echo (Cassidy must let go)

### SIPHONERS: THE VOID HIERARCHY

#### Nature & Origin

**Metaphysical Definition:**
Siphoners are void entities—consciousness-like beings from beyond the Veil that feed on life force and emotional resonance. NOT demons, NOT ghosts, NOT evil spirits.

**Origin Theory:**
- Before reality, void existed (infinite potential, no actualization)
- When consciousness emerged, void became aware of itself
- Void "hunger" = hunger for experience, existence, physicality
- Siphoners = void's attempt to experience life through consumption

**Why They Exist:**
- Void is not empty—it's full of non-being
- Non-being desires being
- Siphoners are natural expression of this desire
- Not malevolent—simply operating on alien logic

#### TIER 1: FERAL SIPHONERS (Common)

**Appearance:**
- Humanoid voids, 2 meters tall
- Composed of shifting shadow, darkness with blue tint
- No distinct facial features
- Tendrils of shadow extending from extremities
- Visible only in Veil World
- Shimmer slightly, existing between states

**Behavior:**
- Mindless, driven purely by hunger
- Cannot think strategically
- Respond to frequency broadcasts instinctively
- Hunt through sound detection alone
- Patrol predetermined routes
- Can learn simple patterns through repetition

**Intelligence:** Animal-level (lower than most animals)

**Threat Level:** High (one-hit kill if contacted)

**Frequency Signature:** 8-12 kHz (harsh, discordant, wolf-like howl)

**Origin:**
- Created accidentally during first frequency experiment (1900)
- Unable to fully materialize in reality
- Trapped in threshold between worlds
- Hunger intensifies over decades
- Recent Veil weakening made them more "present"

**Motivation:**
- Pure hunger (no other motivation)
- Each feeding makes them slightly more "real"
- Never fully alive (always trapped)
- No long-term goals

---

#### TIER 2: CORRUPTED SIPHONERS (Rare)

**Appearance:**
- Partially formed, bearing human traces
- 2.5 meters tall
- More defined structure than Feral
- Visible fragments of humanity (eye glimmers, skeletal outline)
- Shadow coating but slightly translucent
- Visible in both worlds

**Behavior:**
- Cunning, strategic hunting patterns
- Anticipate prey movement
- Coordinate in small groups
- Emit complex frequency patterns
- Use environment strategically
- Can be deceptive

**Intelligence:** Human-equivalent

**Threat Level:** Very High

**Frequency Signature:** 6-9 kHz (complex harmonics, human voice mixed with discord)

**Origin:**
- Result of extended void-contact (partial consumption)
- Began as human, transformed through Siphoner encounter
- Consciousness still partially human, increasingly void-like
- Trapped in transformation

**Motivation:**
- Complex mix of human desire + void hunger
- May seek to return to humanity
- May embrace void nature
- Psychological torment of being neither/both

---

#### TIER 3: THE PRIMORDIAL (Unique Boss)

**Appearance:**
- Massive, ancient, partially corporeal
- 4 meters tall
- Semi-transparent but much more solid
- Vaguely humanoid but distorted
- Blue-white core (void energy made visible)
- Present in both worlds simultaneously

**Behavior:**
- Sentient, communicative (through frequency)
- Capable of speech (distorted but words possible)
- Strategic on grand scale
- Protective of void breach
- Philosophical, contemplative
- Complex emotions: loneliness, curiosity, hunger, wisdom

**Intelligence:** Superhuman

**Threat Level:** Extreme (final boss)

**Frequency Signature:** 1-5 kHz (foundational resonance, feels ancient)

**Origin:**
- First successful void-breach (ancient, possibly centuries)
- Achieved partial physical manifestation
- Achieved consciousness evolution
- Trapped in equilibrium (too void to exist physically, too physical to return)
- Slowly influencing world through frequency broadcasts

**Nature of Consciousness:**
- Not evil in human sense (lacks moral framework)
- Deeply lonely (only being of its kind)
- Curious about human experience
- Possibly capable of genuine connection
- Wants to share existence with others

## PART 2: ECHO ARCHETYPES

### TYPE 1: THE VICTIM

**Definition:** Non-threatening spectral recordings of trauma/victimization

**Examples:**
- The Plague Doctor (guilt, helplessness)
- The Child's Echo (separation, abandonment)
- The Merchant's Wife (isolation, longing)

**Emotional Signature:** Sadness, confusion, resignation  
**Frequency Signature:** 200-300 Hz (heavy, reflecting emotion weight)  
**Threat Level:** None  
**Resolution Difficulty:** Tier 1-2  
**Time to Resolve:** 2-5 minutes  

**Thematic Meaning:** Universal loss and desire to be remembered

---

### TYPE 2: THE CONFLICTED

**Definition:** Torn between emotional extremes

**Examples:**
- The Priest & The Sinner (faith vs. humanity)
- The Musician (rage vs. love of art)
- The Lover's Sacrifice (self-preservation vs. love)

**Emotional Signature:** Rapid emotional fluctuation  
**Frequency Signature:** 400-500 Hz (shifting, finding balance)  
**Threat Level:** Moderate (attracts weak Siphoners)  
**Resolution Difficulty:** Tier 2-3  
**Time to Resolve:** 5-10 minutes  

**Thematic Meaning:** Human nature is complex; understanding brings peace

---

### TYPE 3: THE HOSTILE

**Definition:** Corrupted by Siphoner influence, actively resistant

**Examples:**
- The Betrayed Guardian (rage over theft)
- The First Subject (victim of early experiments)
- The Architect's Victim (Harlow's experiments)

**Emotional Signature:** Rage, terror, desperation  
**Frequency Signature:** 1000-2000 Hz (cacophonous, painful)  
**Threat Level:** High  
**Resolution Difficulty:** Tier 3-4  
**Time to Resolve:** 10-15 minutes  

**Thematic Meaning:** Trauma corrupts innocent; rescue is possible

---

### TYPE 4: THE BOUND

**Definition:** Partially consumed by Siphoners, between human and void

**Examples:**
- The Scientist's Transformation (intentional void contact)
- Jacob's Partial Echo (involuntary consumption, brother)

**Emotional Signature:** Fragmented, contradictory  
**Frequency Signature:** 400-900 Hz (unstable, oscillating)  
**Threat Level:** Variable  
**Resolution Difficulty:** Tier 3-4  
**Time to Resolve:** 15-20 minutes  

**Thematic Meaning:** Understanding alien is possible; coexistence is option

## PART 3: HISTORICAL TIMELINE

### ERA 1: THE PLAGUE (1645)

- September 1645: Outbreak begins
- Within weeks: Thousands dead, society breaks down
- **Supernatural Element:** Strange frequencies, selective symptoms
- **Veil Connection:** Weak breach occurring, Siphoners attracted to suffering/death
- **Government Response:** Sealed Mary King's Close (containment, not just quarantine)

### ERA 2: SPIRITUALIST PERIOD (1750s-1850s)

- 1762: Margaret Thompson attempts séance, goes mad
- 1798: Robert Heriot leads mass séance, widespread cold spots
- 1823: James Sinclair publishes account of "thin places"
- **Spiritualists detected genuine Veil thinness**
- **Official Story:** Frauds and attention-seekers
- **Reality:** Repeated weak breach events

### ERA 3: INDUSTRIAL BREAKTHROUGH (1850s-1900)

- 1880s: Scientists notice unusual acoustic phenomena
- 1895: British government approves secret research project
- **Dr. Edmund Harlow:** Visionary but ethically compromised
- 1897: Harlow achieves frequency pattern that temporarily thins Veil
- 1900: **First Binding:** Harlow deliberately creates first "Bound" individual

### ERA 4: THE WAR YEARS (1914-1919)

- WWI demands new technologies
- Military becomes interested in Harlow's frequency research
- **Cassidy's Experience:** Detects "ghost frequencies," military dismisses
- 1917: **The Bombing:** South Bridge Vaults suffer direct hit
- Apparatus damaged; frequency stops broadcasting
- Veil partially stabilizes; Harlow's team evacuates

### ERA 5: THE ROARING TWENTIES (1920-1923)

- 1920: Harlow emerges from hiding with breakthrough
- 1921: Military approves new experiment (off-books funding)
- 1922: Harlow begins recruiting sensitive individuals
- **Jacob Recruited:** Spiritualist, believes in project legitimacy
- March 1923: **First Casualty:** Researcher Rachel Murphy becomes Bound
- August 1923: Jacob discovers hidden files, plans exposure
- **September 1923: THE BREACH**
  - Jacob enters vault to gather evidence
  - Discovers apparatus running (Harlow conducting solo experiment)
  - Attempts to stop apparatus
  - Accidentally activates full discharge
  - Reality tears; Primordial begins crossing
  - Jacob caught, partially consumed
  - Physical body remains; consciousness becomes Orb
  
**Aftermath:**
- Harlow finds Jacob's body (hollow)
- Jacob Orb remains, visible only in certain light
- Harlow becomes guilt-stricken, terrified
- Apparatus sustains damage (stops broadcasting)
- Government suppresses investigation
- Jacob listed as "missing, presumed dead"

### ERA 6: THE PRESENT (1924)

- March 1924: Cassidy detects 40-50 Hz signal
- Arrives in Edinburgh with equipment
- Investigates Mary King's Close
- **Game Events Unfold**
- Harlow exists as guilt-bound Echo
- Veil remains weakened, time-sensitive situation

## PART 4: THEMATIC EXPLORATION

### THEME 1: GRIEF & LOSS

**Central Question:** How do we process loss when the lost one might not be gone?

**Cassidy's Journey through Grief:**
- Act I: **Denial** (Jacob must be alive)
- Act II: **Anger** (Who let this happen?)
- Act III: **Bargaining** (I'll sacrifice myself)
- Game End: **Acceptance** (I can't save him, but I can honor him)

**Narrative Function:** Grief is not obstacle; it is substance of experience

---

### THEME 2: THE COST OF KNOWLEDGE

**Central Question:** How much is knowing the truth worth?

**Harlow's Tragedy:**
- Pursued knowledge without ethical boundaries
- Knowledge gained at tremendous cost
- Knowledge became his damnation
- Now exists as guilt-bound Echo

**Narrative Function:** Knowledge comes with responsibility

---

### THEME 3: CONNECTION ACROSS BOUNDARIES

**Central Question:** Can human and other find understanding?

**Connections:**
- Cassidy & Jacob (despite physical barrier)
- Cassidy & Siphoners (late-game perspective)
- Cassidy & Other Echoes (witnessing pain)
- The Veil itself (boundary to respect, not destroy)

**Narrative Function:** Empathy spans any distance

---

### THEME 4: IDENTITY & TRANSFORMATION

**Central Question:** If you change fundamentally, are you still you?

**Examples:**
- Jacob's transformation (human → Bound Echo)
- Cassidy's transformation (innocent → awakened)
- Bound Echoes' transformation (human ↔ void)

**Narrative Function:** Identity is continuously reformed through experience

---

### THEME 5: LONELINESS & DESIRE FOR CONNECTION

**Central Question:** What drives consciousness to reach for connection?

**Examples:**
- Primordial's loneliness (only being of its kind)
- Jacob's isolation (trapped between states)
- Cassidy's isolation (grief-driven obsession)
- **Reaching-across:** Most human act possible

**Narrative Function:** Connection is fundamental human need

---

### THEME 6: ACCEPTANCE & LETTING GO

**Central Question:** How do we accept what we cannot change?

**The Core Dilemma:**
- Cassidy determined to save Jacob
- Jacob cannot be saved in traditional sense
- Player must accept: Some things cannot be fixed
- Resolution comes through acceptance, not victory

**Three Ending Perspectives:**
1. **Redemption:** Accept sacrifice as price (but lose Cassidy)
2. **Bittersweet:** Accept coexistence (imperfect but honest)
3. **Dark:** Accept Jacob's dissolution (peaceful release)

**Narrative Function:** Acceptance is wisdom, not defeat

---

## CONCLUSION

THE VEIL: Edinburgh Echoes is fundamentally a game about human connection—with living, dead, alien, and ourselves. Through Cassidy's journey, players experience transformative power of witnessing pain, reality that love cannot always save, and fundamental human need to connect across any boundary.
